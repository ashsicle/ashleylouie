ABOUT
Ashley Louie is a spatial thinker with a passion for data visualization and design of urban environments for people.

SPATIAL DATA
With today's rapidly changing technology, we are surrounded by a wealth of data and information. Data, documented in a visual language or codified in traces of iterative permutations, is particularly impactful in informing the programming, policy, and design of spaces. Although it will not generate design by itself, spatial data can be powerful tool in communicating ideas and supporting design arguments.

CONNECTIVE FABRIC
As cities continue to grow in density and complexity through an increasingly virtual network of communication, the connective fabric of public spaces will become critical in sustaining urban life by providing a sense of identity and community through place. Understanding a place through a variety of scales reveals interconnected system challenges, while providing opportunities for design interventions.

SOCIAL ADVOCACY
Recognizing the social responsibility as a leader and designer, sharing knowledge and skills to help facilitate a collective design process is crucial to changing the city. Asserting agency in urban design begins with a vision that prioritizes the people and needs within a community. If we can communicate with stakeholders, innovate resources, and implement physical interventions, we can empower design for social advocacy.
